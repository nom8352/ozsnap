## Design Context

### Users
Australian residents and domestic travellers checking local conditions before leaving home, taking a weekend trip, or planning an outdoor photo session. Most visits are mobile and time-sensitive. The primary job is to decide what to wear and when the best evening light will occur without reading a conventional weather report.

### Brand Personality
Practical, editorial, and quietly confident. OZSNAP should feel locally informed and visually considered without becoming precious, technical, or overly playful. The interface should create immediate trust, followed by the small delight of seeing weather translated into clothing and photographic light.

### Aesthetic Direction
Australian editorial field guide in light mode. Use an expressive editorial display typeface with a restrained sans-serif body face, deep eucalyptus as the dominant tone, sun yellow as a light signal, and coral only for high-value emphasis. Photography and outfit illustrations should carry the visual identity. Avoid generic weather dashboards, glass effects, gradients, glowing accents, excessive pills, symmetrical SaaS card grids, and camera-shutter cliches.

### Design Principles
1. Answer the first question in seconds: city, temperature, outfit, and the next useful action must be immediately visible.
2. Translate data into decisions: weather values support plain-language clothing and photography guidance rather than becoming the product themselves.
3. Reveal depth progressively: keep the current-day view decisive, then offer forecast, best light, planning, and education in that order.
4. Feel distinctly Australian without resorting to tourist imagery or slang-heavy copy.
5. Keep the experience flagship-fast: locally hosted assets, stable dimensions, accessible interactions, and no decorative work that harms performance.

### Quality Bar
Flagship public release. Desktop and mobile should feel intentionally composed, all interaction and failure states must be complete, and technical SEO should be ready for production indexing.
