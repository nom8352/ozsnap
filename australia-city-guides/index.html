Forecast first, local context second

Use the city guide as an adjustment, not a forecast

Start with the live feels-like temperature, rain chance, wind and UV for your selected OZSNAP city. Then use the local note to account for the part a city-wide number cannot describe: harbour exposure, a fast wind shift, tropical humidity, a dry evening drop or a mountain influence.

OZSNAP’s location button selects the nearest supported city. It does not provide exact-coordinate weather, official observations or warnings. Conditions can differ across a large metropolitan area, particularly near coastlines, ranges and open water.

For an all-day plan, check three moments.Compare the morning start, warmest part of the afternoon and expected finish. Carry the smallest removable layer that covers the largest change.

NSW, VIC, QLD and ACT

East coast and south-east city notes

Sydney · NSW

Harbour exposure changes comfort

A southerly can make harbour edges feel cooler, especially after sunset. Carry a light wind-resistant layer for ferries, headlands and waterside finishes. For photos, compare the open harbour breeze with more sheltered urban edges.

Melbourne · VIC

Keep the outer layer close

A wind shift can change a mild day quickly. Removable layers are more useful than dressing only for the afternoon maximum. Build extra flexibility into outdoor portrait times when wind and showers are moving through.

Brisbane · QLD

Humidity can hold after sunset

Breathable layers usually remain useful into the evening. A light rain shell can handle passing showers without adding insulation. For portraits, look for shade and airflow before adding more equipment. Parents planning before the baby arrives can also consider Sweetlife maternity photography in North Lakes.

Gold Coast · QLD

Beachfront wind needs its own check

The foreshore is often more exposed than streets a few blocks inland. Secure hats and choose a layer that closes at the front. A sheltered beach access or park edge can preserve good light with easier portrait conditions.

Canberra · ACT

Clear evenings cool quickly

A warm afternoon can lead into a much colder finish, particularly outside summer. Pack a proper evening layer rather than relying on the daytime temperature. Arrive early for hilltop photos so the walk back is not rushed after dark.

WA, SA and Tasmania

Dry warmth, sea breezes and southern cold

Perth · WA

Plan for the afternoon sea breeze

Warm, dry conditions can change quickly along the coast when the breeze arrives. Light clothing may still suit the temperature, but a wind layer helps for a late beach walk or exposed shoot. Check the actual session hour rather than the daily average.

Adelaide · SA

Dry days can lose warmth quickly

Heat may linger through summer evenings, while other seasons cool faster once the sun drops. Sun protection and water matter on hot days; a compact layer earns its place when the finish extends beyond sunset.

Hobart · TAS

Wind can lower the apparent temperature

River and mountain exposure can make conditions feel colder than the air reading. Use warm layers that also block wind. For photography, an open outlook may have better light but a sheltered lower position may keep people comfortable longer.

Northern QLD and the Top End

Heat, humidity and fast showers

Cairns · QLD

Use quick-dry, breathable layers

Tropical humidity and passing showers make heavy clothing impractical. Keep rain protection light and ventilated, and protect equipment from sudden moisture. Earlier sessions may be more comfortable for people than the warmest part of the day.

Darwin · NT

Sun protection matters more than insulation

Light fabrics, shade and water are the default in humid weather. Plan an indoor or sheltered alternative when showers and heat affect comfort. For sunset work, arrive with enough time to manage changing cloud rather than relying on a single colour forecast.

In tropical conditions, a lower temperature after rain does not always mean a cool-feeling day. Humidity can slow evaporation, so choose airflow and dry clothing before adding warmth.

One city, three decisions

Build a practical city-day plan

  1. Select the nearest supported city. Treat it as broad metropolitan guidance, especially near the edge of the forecast area.
  2. Set clothing from feels-like temperature. Adjust for whether you run cold, will be active or expect to stand still.
  3. Check the local exposure. Coast, harbour, river, hill and open park locations usually need a closer wind check.
  4. Compare the finish time. A layer that seems unnecessary at lunch may be the right choice after sunset.
  5. Match the sky to the photograph. Choose portrait softness, sunset colour or blue-hour structure before choosing the session time.

Quick answers

Common city-planning questions

Which Australian cities does OZSNAP support?

Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Gold Coast, Cairns, Perth, Adelaide, Hobart, Canberra and Darwin. Use my location selects whichever of these is nearest.

Are the city forecasts exact for my suburb?

No. They are city-level planning guidance. Local terrain, distance from the coast and moving weather can create differences across a metropolitan area.

Can I use the city guide for weather warnings?

No. OZSNAP helps with ordinary clothing and photography planning. Use official local sources for warnings, emergencies, hazardous weather and access decisions.
